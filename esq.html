What Makes a Good Leader?

A good leader is someone who people look to for support. Leaders are reliable, respectful, positive, and logical. They are excellent listeners and communicators. Furthermore, good leaders are people with a vision. They can strategically plan and organize their team's activities, taking every member's strengths and abilities into account.

Leaders also constantly encourage and check in with their group members. They are motivating, and rather than leading by force, their dedication, commitment, and ambition inspire others' contributions. They earn the respect and trust of the team, showing gratitude and empathy whenever necessary.

When resilient leaders inevitably encounter difficult situations, they are able to adapt and overcome these obstacles. Leaders actively enable and encourage teamwork for improved productivity and communication. They are also very courageous, being unafraid to provide feedback, make change, and take leadership in the first place.

Overall, good leaders possess high intelligence, emotional intelligence, and open-mindedness. They can effectively create strategic plans, communicate and manage people, and accept different perspectives/ideas.

Activity Idea

Website Application: Calculator

General Information

  • Description: Walk learners through the process of creating a functional website calculator.
  • Objective: Teach participants how to think methodically/logically and program simple website applications. Introduce the most widely used web-development languages (HTML, CSS, and JavaScript (JS)).
  • Estimated Duration: 2-3 hours.

Learning

  • Overview: Teach participants project file structure and how the files interact to display the website.
  • HTML: The content of the website. In this case, the calculator (numbers, operations, display screen, etc.). Show usage of basic HTML tags including 'html', 'head', 'body', 'table', and 'input', as well as stylesheet (CSS) and script (JS) linking.
  • CSS: The 'presentation' or 'look' of the website. For a calculator, this includes the background colour, and the calculator and button's sizes and colours. Explain how selectors are used with classes and IDs to pick elements to style.
  • JS: The behaviour and functionality of the website. Here, the JavaScript handles the actions: the input of numbers and operators, the calculations, and the displaying of the result.
